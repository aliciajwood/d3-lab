DISPARITIES IN THE U.S. PUBLIC EDUCATION SYSTEM

Analysts at WalletHub conducted a study in 2022 that evaluated all 50 states and the District of Columbia across 15 different metrics measuring the quality of their public education system. Connecticut, Massachussetts, and New Jersey came out on top with the best quality education, whilst New Mexico, Alaska, and Louisiana were found to have the worst. 4

Use the map below to explore the differences in our public education systems across all 50 states and the District of Columbia through these 5 key metrics:

  • Graduation Rates: U.S. Public High School 4-year Adjusted Cohort Graduation Rate (2018-2019) 2
  • Class Size: Number of Students Enrolled Per Teacher in U.S. Public Schools (2020-2021) 1
  • Teacher Pay: Average Salary for U.S. Public School Teachers (2020-2021) 1
  • Staffing: Number of Underqualified Teachers Per 10,000 Students in U.S. Public Schools (differs by state ranging from 2020-2021 to 2014-2015) 3
  • Spending: Expenditures Per Student in Fall Enrollment in U.S. Public Schools (2019-2020) 1
